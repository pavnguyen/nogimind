# Technique Research Policy

This document outlines the guidelines for researching and gathering technical data for the NoGi Mind application. Our goal is to maintain a professional, modern, and high-fidelity technical database while strictly adhering to ethical research practices and intellectual property rights.

## 1. Ethical Research Guidelines

### Allowed Sources & Methods
- **Discovery Signals**: Use public YouTube titles, descriptions, and thumbnails to identify trending techniques or common teaching points.
- **Competition Analysis**: Observe and summarize techniques from public competition footage (ADCC, IBJJF, etc.).
- **Terminology**: Use common grappling terminology and general industry names for techniques.
- **Original Summaries**: All technical descriptions, mechanical explanations, and cues must be written originally by the NoGi Mind team.
- **Internal Knowledge**: Leverage the existing `/content` folder and internal technical expertise.

### Prohibited Practices
- **No Direct Copying**: Never copy transcripts from videos, paid instructionals, or books.
- **No Proprietary Wording**: Do not use unique, trademarked phrasing or proprietary instructional structures as if they were our own.
- **No Copyright Infringement**: Do not copy content from BJJGraph, other grappling apps, or paid subscription sites.
- **No Piracy**: Do not link to or embed pirated video content.
- **No Shallow Filler**: Avoid generic or AI-generated "fluff." Every detail must be practical and execution-detail precise.

## 2. Content Originality

Every technical entry in NoGi Mind must be a "masterclass" in original synthesis. We take the "what" from the grappling meta and provide our own "how" and "why."

- **Mechanical Why**: Explain the physics and leverage in our own words.
- **Cues**: Develop original, actionable cues (e.g., "Heavy hip on the ankle line" instead of "Push his leg").
- **Multi-lingual Consistency**: Ensure technical precision is maintained across English, Vietnamese, and French.

## 3. Recording Research

For every technique researched or added, a corresponding entry must be made in `docs/MODERN_NOGI_RESEARCH_NOTES.md`. These notes are for internal reference and audit purposes only and should not be exposed in the production app.

- **Note Format**:
    - Technique/System Name
    - Source URL/Title (for discovery reference)
    - Source Type (Public Video, Article, Competition, etc.)
    - Short Original Summary
    - Why Relevant to Modern Meta
    - Human Review Flag

## 4. Safety & Risk Assessment

Techniques must be categorized by risk level. High-risk or safety-critical techniques (e.g., Heel Hooks, Small Joint Manipulations, Neck Cranks) must include explicit safety aborts and tap-early protocols.
